Getting to Know Tim Burton

About

Timothy Walter Burton (known as Tim Burton) is an American film producer, writer, and director. His work is quite distinct, being influenced largely by German Expressionism and characterized into horror and gothic fantasy.

Filmography

Since his start as a producer in 1979, Tim Burton has worked on video documentaries, music videos, tv mini-series, shorts, and full-length films. Some of the more well-known productions he has been involved with include:

  • Edward Scissorhands
  • Batman Returns
  • Batman Forever
  • James and the Giant Peach
  • Frankenweenie
  • Alice Through the Looking Glass
  • Dumbo
  • Charlie and the Chocolate Factory
  • among others...

Personal Favorites:

The Nightmare Before Christmas

The Nightmare Before Christmas (1993) is a film that follows Jack Skellington, the pumpkin king of Halloweentown, as he grows tired of the life he currently lives. Along with town members, he is responsible for bringing fright to the world through Halloween. As he roams the forest, reflecting on his life, he stumbles upon Christmastown. With its lights and small toys, Jack becomes enchanted and looks to take control of Christmas. Throughout the film, we watch as Halloweentown prepares for Christmas by creating toys and a sled with reindeer on top of kidnapping Santa Claus. In the end, Jack recognizes that he had taken his life for granted and shows a new-found appreciation for life and those around him.

Corpse Bride

The Corpse Bride (2005) is a film that follows Victor, a shy groom who's family has arranged his marriage to Victoria, as he grows nervous about the life he is being forced into. As he tries to walk off the nerves in the forest, he ends up being sucked into the land of the dead where he meets and inadvertently marries Emily, the Corpse Bride. We watch as Victor tries to get back to the land of the living before his Victoria marries another man, and in the meantime learns what it means to truly love and what he wants from his life.
